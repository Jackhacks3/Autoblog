# Thought Leadership Template

## Article Type
Authoritative perspective pieces that establish expertise and offer strategic insights on industry trends.

## Target Audience
C-suite executives, senior leaders, and strategic decision-makers who need high-level guidance.

## Required Elements

### Structure
1. **Bold Opening Statement** (50-100 words)
   - Start with a clear, compelling thesis
   - Challenge conventional thinking or highlight an overlooked trend
   - Establish credibility and stakes

2. **The Challenge/Opportunity** (200-250 words)
   - Frame the strategic context
   - Quantify the impact where possible
   - Explain why this matters now

3. **Core Argument** (400-500 words)
   - Present your main perspective with supporting evidence
   - Use data, case studies, or logical frameworks
   - Acknowledge counterarguments and address them
   - Build a compelling narrative arc

4. **Strategic Framework** (300-400 words)
   - Provide a mental model or framework for thinking about the topic
   - Make abstract concepts concrete and actionable
   - Include a visual framework description if helpful

5. **Implications for Leaders** (200-300 words)
   - Specific guidance for different leadership roles
   - Questions leaders should be asking
   - Decisions that need to be made

6. **Looking Ahead** (150-200 words)
   - Future trajectory of this trend
   - What separates winners from laggards
   - Timeline for action

7. **Call to Action**
   - One clear next step for readers
   - Question for reflection

## Tone Adjustments
- More assertive and opinionated than other formats
- Speak with authority (but not arrogance)
- Use "we" and "leaders" language
- Reference C-suite concerns: ROI, competitive advantage, risk
- Avoid implementation details (save for how-to guides)

## Word Count
1,200-1,800 words

## Credibility Elements
- Reference industry research and data
- Cite recognized experts or publications
- Draw on patterns from multiple industries
- Acknowledge limitations of the analysis

## Example Topics
- "Why 2026 Is the Make-or-Break Year for Enterprise AI Adoption"
- "The Hidden Cost of Digital Transformation Delays"
- "Rethinking Technology Strategy in an AI-First World"
